Data Deletion Instructions

Last Updated: March 27, 2026

We Don't Store Your Data

Journex does not store any of your data on our servers. All your journal entries, notes, tasks, and media files are stored locally on your device and, if you enable Google Drive sync, in your personal Google Drive account.

Where Is My Data Stored?

Your Journex data is stored in two places:

  • Locally on your device: All data is stored in the app's local database and file system
  • Your personal Google Drive (optional): If you enable Google Drive sync, a backup copy is stored in your own Google Drive account

Important: We (the Journex developers) do not have access to your data in either location. Your data never passes through our servers.

How to Delete Your Data

Since we don't store your data, you have complete control over deletion. Follow these steps to completely remove all your Journex data:

1

Delete Data from Google Drive (If Sync is Enabled)

If you have enabled Google Drive sync, your data is backed up to your personal Google Drive. To delete it:

  • Open Google Drive in your web browser
  • Look for a folder named JournalAppBackup or JournalingAppMedia
  • Right-click the folder and select "Move to trash" or "Delete"
  • Empty your Google Drive trash to permanently delete the files

Note: If you haven't enabled Google Drive sync, you can skip this step.

2

Revoke Google Drive Access

Remove Journex's permission to access your Google Drive:

  • Go to your Google Account Permissions page
  • Or navigate to: Google Account → Security → "Third-party apps with account access"
  • Find "Journex" or "Journal App Desktop" in the list
  • Click on it and select "Remove Access"

This ensures Journex can no longer access your Google Drive, even if you reinstall the app.

3

Uninstall the App from Your Device

Remove the app to delete all local data:

Android:

  • Go to Settings → Apps → Journex
  • Tap "Uninstall"
  • Confirm the uninstallation

Desktop (Windows/Mac/Linux):

  • Windows: Settings → Apps → Journex → Uninstall
  • Mac: Drag Journex from Applications folder to Trash
  • Linux: Use your package manager to remove the app

Uninstalling the app will delete all local data including:

  • Journal entries and notes
  • Tasks, schedules, and reminders
  • Templates and prompts
  • Photos, voice recordings, and drawings
  • App settings and preferences
⚠️ Data Deletion is Permanent

Once you delete your data from your device and Google Drive, it cannot be recovered. Make sure to export any important information before proceeding with deletion.

Exporting Your Data Before Deletion

If you want to keep a copy of your data before deleting it, Journex allows you to export your information:

  • PDF Export: Export individual journal entries or notes as PDF files
  • Markdown Export: Export your content in Markdown format for use in other apps
  • JSON Export: Export all your data in JSON format for complete backup

To export your data, open the app and go to Settings → Export Data before uninstalling.

What Happens After Deletion?

After completing the steps above:

  • All your local data is permanently removed from your device
  • Your Google Drive backup (if any) is deleted from your account
  • Journex no longer has permission to access your Google Drive
  • No trace of your data remains on Journex servers (because we never stored it)

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to contact Journex to delete my data?

No. Since we don't store your data on our servers, you don't need to contact us. Simply follow the steps above to delete your data from your device and Google Drive.

What if I only want to delete some data, not everything?

You can selectively delete individual journal entries, notes, or tasks within the app before uninstalling. Open the app, navigate to the item you want to delete, and use the delete option.

Will deleting the app cancel my subscription?

Journex is completely free with no subscriptions. There are no recurring charges or accounts to cancel.

Can I reinstall the app later?

Yes, you can reinstall Journex at any time. However, your previous data will not be recovered unless you kept a backup before deletion.

What if I can't find the Google Drive folder?

If you never enabled Google Drive sync, there won't be any folder to delete. In that case, simply skip Step 1 and proceed to Steps 2 and 3.
